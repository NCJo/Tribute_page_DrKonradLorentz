Dr. Konrad Lorenz

The Father of Ethology.

"I have found the missing link between the higher ape and civilized man; it is we."

Lorenz studied instinctive behavior in animals with greylag geese.

Dr. Lorenz's Life and Achievements Timeline:

  • 1903 - Born in Vienna, Austria-Hungary
  • 1941 - In WWII, he was recruited as a medic by the German Army
  • 1922 - Began premedical curriculum at Columbia University
  • 1923 - Returned to Vienna to continue his study at University of Vienna
  • 1928 - Graduated as Doctor of Medicine (MD) and became assistant professor at Institute of Anatomy
  • 1933 - Finished zoological studies and received his second doctorate (PhD)
  • 1936 - Met great friend and lifetime colleague Nikolass Tinbergen, they studied wide-range species of geese on their instinctive behaviors
  • 1940 - Began teaching psychology as a professor at University of Königsberg
  • 1941 - Was drafted into Wehrmacht. He sought to be a motorcycle mechanic, but instead he was assigned as military psychologist
  • 1944 - Was sent to Eastern Front, where he was captured and spent four years as a Soviet prisoner of war
  • 1958 - Was transferred to Max Planck Institute for Behavioral Physiology in Seewiesen
  • 1969 - First recipient to ever award Prix mondial Cino Del Duca
  • 1973 - Received Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine "for discoveries in individual and social behavior patterns" with two other important early ethologists, Nikolaas Tinbergen and Karl von Frisch
  • 1973 - Retired from Max Planck Institute
  • 1989 - Died at age of 85 in Altenberg
